    Health Insurance

    Under the age of 65, most employers offer health insurance coverage as part of their benefits package. For those outside of traditional employment or looking for additional coverage, I can help you explore your options. The policies available on the Health Insurance Marketplace provides a subsidy for eligible individuals and families to cover a portion of their premiums when there is no employer-paid portion. For dental and vision coverage, Delta Dental of Iowa provides comprehensive plans on an extensive network. Outside of marketplaces open enrollment periods, or any special enrollment periods, short-term health insurance may be the best fit to patch gaps in coverage.

    Medicare is a federal program that provides health insurance for individuals aged 65 and older, as well as certain younger individuals with disabilities. It consists of different parts, including Part A (hospital insurance), Part B (medical insurance), Part C (Medicare Advantage), and Part D (prescription drug coverage). Medigap plans cover care that Original Medicare doesn't. The exact coverage options are identical between carriers, published on Medicare.gov. Once eligible for Medicare, you must maintain creditable coverage or carry a late enrollment penalty indefinitely. That doesn't mean you're stuck with Medicare benefits handled by the federal government, Medicare Advantage (Part C) is administered by private insurance companies. Many Medicare Advantage plans offer additional benefits beyond what Original Medicare provides, without an additional premium. The most appropriate option for your needs depends on which plan's drug formulary and provider network best meets your care needs. Take a look at available options.
